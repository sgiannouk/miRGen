DIANA Lab

At the DIANA Lab, we develop advanced algorithms, databases, and tools to interpret and archive genomic data through a systems biology lens. Our work focuses on integrating complex biological information into comprehensive, predictive models that span multiple levels of biological detail.

Currently, we place particular emphasis on studying microRNAs (miRNAs) and protein-coding genes. MiRNAs are abundant in mammalian systems and play a crucial role in regulating development and other fundamental biological processes.

Our research activities range from:

  • Analyzing gene expression regulation using deep sequencing data
  • Annotating miRNA regulatory elements and their targets
  • Investigating the roles of miRNAs in diverse diseases

By bridging computational innovation with biological insight, DIANA Lab contributes to a deeper understanding of gene regulation and its impact on health and disease.
